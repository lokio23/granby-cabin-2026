Smash Burgers

Wed May 20 · Arrival Night · 20 min · Cast Iron Skillet

You just drove from Fort Collins. You're finally here. Simple food, cold drinks, and the cabin is yours for nine days.

Ingredients (serves 2 — 2 burgers each)

  • 1 lb ground beef, 80/20
  • 4 slices American cheese
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter (bun toasting)
  • Kosher salt + fresh cracked pepper
  • Mayo, yellow mustard, pickles
  • Lettuce, tomato, sliced onion

Method

  1. Divide beef into 4 equal balls (~3.75 oz). Do not overwork — balls should barely hold together.
  2. Heat cast iron over medium-high 3–4 min until a water droplet vanishes instantly. Toast buns cut-side down in butter in a second pan; set aside.
  3. Place 2 beef balls on the hot skillet. Within 2–3 seconds, smash down hard with a flat spatula (use parchment between spatula and beef).
  4. Press to ¼" thick, ~4–5" wide. Season top immediately with salt and pepper.
  5. Do not touch for 90 seconds. This is where the crust forms.
  6. Flip decisively. Lay a slice of American cheese on each patty immediately.
  7. Cook 30 sec. Cover with a plate or lid for 15 sec to steam-melt the cheese. Remove.
  8. Repeat for batch 2. Assemble and rest 1–2 min before eating.

First Side 90 sec Don't touch it
Second Side 30 sec + 15 sec lid for cheese

Side: Vinegar Coleslaw (serves 2)

Dressing can be made tonight at home. Dress the cabbage at the cabin on arrival — no cooking required.

  • 3 cups pre-shredded cabbage + carrot mix
  • ⅓ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp water
  1. Whisk vinegar, sugar, salt, pepper, and water until sugar dissolves.
  2. Toss with cabbage and carrots until every strand is coated.
  3. Let sit 15 min before serving — cabbage softens slightly.
  4. Taste and adjust: more sugar if too sharp, more vinegar if too flat.

Braised Chuck Roast

Thu May 21 · First Full Day · ~4 hrs · Dutch Oven

First full day in the mountains. The roast goes in at noon, the cabin fills with the smell of wine and herbs all afternoon, and dinner is ready when you are.

Day-of Timing — Target 7 PM Dinner

  1. 11:30 AM Pull roast from cooler to come to room temp
  2. Noon Sear roast, build braising base; into oven by 12:30 PM
  3. 2:00 PM Flip roast
  4. 3:30–4 PM Start checking temp — target 195–205°F
  5. 4:00–4:30 PM Roast done. Rest 10 min, shred, strain liquid
  6. 5:30 PM Roast garlic for mashed potatoes (40 min at 375°F)
  7. 6:30 PM Boil and mash potatoes
  8. 7:00 PM Dinner — open the wine

Ingredients (serves 2 + 2–3 leftover meals)

  • 3 lb boneless beef chuck roast (well-marbled)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, rough chopped
  • 2 large carrots, cut in 2" pieces
  • 2 celery stalks, cut in 2" pieces
  • 4–5 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine (same bottle you'll drink)
  • 1½ cups beef stock
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1½ tsp dried thyme (or 3 sprigs fresh)
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary · ½ tsp ground cumin
  • 2 bay leaves · Kosher salt + pepper

Method

  1. Remove roast from cooler 30 min ahead. Pat completely dry with paper towels — moisture kills the sear.
  2. Preheat oven to 275°F. Heat Dutch oven over medium-high, add olive oil.
  3. Sear roast — 4 min per side, do not move it — until a deep brown crust forms. Remove to a plate.
  4. Pour off all but 1 tbsp oil. Add onion, carrot, celery. Cook 3–4 min until slightly softened.
  5. Add garlic, cook 30 sec. Add tomato paste, stir constantly 1 min to caramelize it.
  6. Pour in wine — scrape up all the brown bits from the bottom. Reduce 1 min.
  7. Add beef stock, Worcestershire, balsamic, bay leaves, herbs, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  8. Nestle roast back in pot. Liquid should come halfway up the sides — not covering the meat.
  9. Cover and place in 275°F oven. At 1.5 hours, flip the roast.
  10. Continue braising 1.5–2 hrs more. Pull when internal temp hits 195–205°F and it shreds easily with two forks. If it resists, give it 20 more minutes.
  11. Rest uncovered 10 min in pot. Transfer to cutting board, shred with two forks. Strain braising liquid; reserve ~2 cups for leftovers.

Total Time 3–4 hrs Flip at 1.5 hrs

Night 1 Plating

Wide shallow bowl. Beef in the center, ladle ½ cup warm braising liquid over the top. Scatter soft braised carrots and onions around the edges. Finish with fresh flat-leaf parsley and a crack of pepper. Crusty bread on the side.
Wine: Pinot Noir or Merlot — use the same bottle to braise and to drink with dinner.

Side: Garlic Mashed Potatoes (serves 2)

Start the garlic roasting at 5:30 PM while the roast rests. Potatoes take 25 minutes total.

Roasted Garlic (40 min — do this first)

  • Cut the top ¼" off a whole garlic head to expose cloves. Drizzle with olive oil, pinch of salt.
  • Wrap in foil. Roast at 375°F for 40 min until cloves are golden and completely soft.
  • Cool 5 min, squeeze cloves into a bowl, mash into a paste with a fork.

Potatoes

  • 1.25 lbs Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled, cut in 1–1.5" chunks
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter, warmed
  • ⅓ cup whole milk or half-and-half, warmed
  • 2 tbsp heavy cream or sour cream (optional)
  • ½ tsp kosher salt · ¼ tsp pepper
  • Fresh chives for finishing
  1. Start potatoes in cold salted water. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer, cook 18–22 min until a knife slides through with zero resistance.
  2. Drain. Return to the warm empty pot, put the lid on, let steam-dry 1 min. This removes excess moisture — the #1 cause of gluey mashed potatoes.
  3. In a small pan, warm the butter + milk + roasted garlic paste together until steaming.
  4. Mash potatoes with a hand masher or ricer. Never use a blender or electric mixer — they make them gluey.
  5. Fold in warm dairy in 3 additions. Stop as soon as it's creamy. Season, fold in chives. Serve within 15 min.

To keep warm: cover and set on lowest heat with a splash of cream stirred in every 10 min.

Casual Friday. The cabin, the mountains outside, good tacos and maybe a margarita.

  • ~¾ lb shredded chuck roast

  • 8 small corn or flour tortillas
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • Shredded cabbage · diced white onion
  • Jalapeño · fresh cilantro · sour cream

  • Salsa or hot sauce
  1. Heat shredded beef + braising liquid in a skillet over medium, 5–7 min until steaming.
  2. Optional crunch: Transfer to a dry cast iron on medium-high for 2–3 min to caramelize the edges. Break up slightly as it crisps.
  3. Warm tortillas directly over the gas flame 15–20 sec per side, or in a dry skillet.

Saturday Brunch: Beef Hash

A slow Saturday morning. No agenda — just crispy potatoes, eggs, and whatever's left of the coffee. Salmon brine starts at 6 PM tonight.

  • ~¾ lb shredded chuck roast
  • 1.5 lbs potatoes, cut in ½" cubes (boil night before and chill)
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 3 tbsp oil or butter
  • Kosher salt + pepper
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce (optional)
  • 4 eggs (2 each)
  • Fresh parsley or chives for finishing
  1. Heat 1.5 tbsp oil in large cast iron over medium-high. Add potatoes in a single layer — don't stir for 3–4 min until golden and crispy on the bottom.
  2. Stir, let cook another 2–3 min. Push to the sides.
  3. Add remaining oil to center. Add diced onion, cook 2 min until softened.
  4. Add shredded beef + Worcestershire. Toss everything together, cook 3–4 min until beef is warm and crispy bits form throughout.
  5. Make 4 small wells. Crack an egg into each well (or fry separately in a second pan). Cover and cook to your preferred doneness.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley or chives.

T-Bone Steaks

Mon–Wed May 25–27 · Confirm Date with Guests · 4 People · Cast Iron + Oven

The one group night. Make the compound butter before guests arrive — it needs to be cold. Everything else comes together fast.

Day-of Timing — Target 7 PM Dinner

  1. Afternoon Make blue cheese dressing · prep wedge salad toppings (bacon, tomatoes, onion) · refrigerate
  2. 6:00 PM Pull steaks from fridge. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  3. 6:30 PM Pat steaks dry, salt all sides, heat cast iron until smoking
  4. 6:40 PM Sear batch 1 (2 steaks) → into oven. Sear batch 2 while batch 1 finishes
  5. 6:55 PM Both batches out of oven, resting under foil, butter baste
  6. 7:00 PM Assemble wedge salads (5 min while steaks rest) · serve

Ingredients (serves 4)

  • 4 T-bone steaks, 1–1.5" thick
  • Kosher salt + fresh cracked pepper
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (avocado or canola)
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed

Garlic Herb Compound Butter (make tonight in Fort Collins — or the morning of)

  • 4 tbsp softened unsalted butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced very fine
  • 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Zest of ½ lemon · pinch of fleur de sel · fresh cracked pepper

Combine, roll in plastic wrap into a log, refrigerate until firm. Slice into coins to serve.

Method — Cast Iron Sear → 400°F Oven

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Pull steaks from cooler 30 min before cooking. Pat completely dry.
  2. Season generously on all sides with kosher salt only — pepper goes on after the sear, not before (it burns at high heat).
  3. Heat cast iron over high heat 4–5 min until it begins to smoke. Add 1 tbsp neutral oil.
  4. Place 2 steaks in pan — don't crowd. Sear 2–3 min without moving until a deep brown crust forms.
  5. Flip. Sear second side 2 min. Stand steak on its bone edge for 30 sec to render the fat strip.
  6. Transfer the entire cast iron skillet to the 400°F oven. Cook 4–6 min until thermometer reads target temp.
  7. Remove skillet from oven. Add butter, thyme, and smashed garlic to the hot pan. Tilt pan and baste steaks with foaming butter for 30–45 sec.
  8. Season tops with fresh cracked pepper. Transfer to warm plate, tent loosely with foil. Rest 5–8 min minimum.
  9. Repeat for steaks 3 and 4. Plate with a coin of compound butter melting on top.

Rest 5–8 min Always. Under foil.

Ask guests their preference. Cook to the most "done" preference; those who want medium-rare go in last.

Wine: Cabernet Sauvignon or Malbec — something with grip and structure to stand up to the bone-in beef.

Side: Wedge Salad (serves 4)

Make the dressing and prep toppings while steaks are in the oven. Assemble during the 5-min rest — perfect timing.

Blue Cheese Dressing (make ahead — even the night before)

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • ¼ cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp buttermilk or whole milk
  • 2 oz crumbled blue cheese
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Whisk all together. It should be tangy and rich. Refrigerate until ready. Makes enough for 4.

Salad

  • 1 head iceberg lettuce, quartered through the core (core holds wedge together)
  • 4 oz crumbled blue cheese, divided
  • 6 strips bacon, cooked crispy and crumbled
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved

  • 2 tbsp fresh chives, minced
  1. Cook bacon in cast iron over medium heat 8–10 min until deeply crispy. Drain, crumble when cool.
  2. Place each lettuce wedge cut-side up on a plate. Spoon dressing generously over each wedge.
  3. Top with crumbled bacon, halved tomatoes, remaining blue cheese, red onion, and chives. Serve immediately.

Salmon Day

Sat May 23 (Brine) → Sun May 24 (Smoke + Dinner) · Traeger + Cast Iron

4a — Candied Smoked Salmon

The Traeger does the work all morning. You just set out the board in the afternoon and pour a drink.

The Brine (Saturday ~6 PM — 10 min)

  • 8 cups water
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • ¾ cup brown sugar, packed
  • ½ cup kosher salt
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated (or ½ tsp ground)
  • 3 bay leaves
  • Optional: 1 tbsp black pepper, pinch red pepper flakes
  1. Combine all brine ingredients. Stir until salt and sugar fully dissolve.
  2. Cool completely before using (don't put warm brine on fish).
  3. Cut 1 lb salmon fillet (skin-on, pin bones removed) into 2–3" pieces.
  4. Submerge salmon in brine, weigh down with a plate. Refrigerate 8–12 hours — overnight is ideal.

Pellicle Formation (Sunday 7:30–9:30 AM — do not skip)

  1. Remove salmon from brine. Rinse under cold water. Pat completely dry.
  2. Place skin-side down on a wire rack. Do NOT stack pieces.
  3. Position a fan to blow air gently across the surface, or place near an open window.
  4. Dry for 2 full hours until the surface is tacky and slightly glossy. This is the pellicle — what smoke sticks to. Rushing this step = uneven smoke coverage.

Smoking on Traeger

  1. Preheat Traeger to 150–170°F (use Super Smoke mode if available). Wood: alder (traditional), cherry, or apple.
  2. Place salmon skin-side down on grates. Start the timer.
  3. Do not open the lid during the first 2 hours.
  4. Check temp at 1:00–1:30 PM. Done when internal temp reads 130–135°F — flesh should be amber-colored, firm, and flake cleanly.
Traeger Temp 150–170°F Low and slow
Smoke Time 3–4 hrs No lid first 2 hrs
Pull Temp 130–135°F Amber, flakes cleanly

The Smoked Salmon Board (Sunday ~2 PM)

After a morning of tending the smoke together, this is your afternoon. Set it out, graze, pour something cold.

  • All the just-smoked candied salmon, broken into rustic pieces
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened to room temp
  • 1 box water crackers or Triscuits
  • 2 tbsp capers, drained
  • ¼ small red onion, sliced paper-thin
  • 4–6 sprigs fresh dill
  • 1 lemon, cut in wedges
  • Optional: sliced cucumber, everything bagel seasoning on the cream cheese
  1. Let smoked salmon rest at room temp 30 min after pulling from the Traeger.
  2. Spread cream cheese onto a board or into a small bowl; drag a spoon through to create swoops.
  3. Break salmon into irregular pieces, arrange alongside the cream cheese.
  4. Scatter capers, red onion slices, and dill around the board. Add crackers and lemon wedges.
  5. Serve at room temperature — cold cream cheese is harder to spread and less flavorful.

How to eat it: Cream cheese on a cracker, piece of salmon, a few capers, sliver of red onion, squeeze of lemon.

4b — Pan-Seared Salmon Dinner (Sunday ~6 PM)

After a full day of smoke and snacking together, dinner is 10 minutes. Crispy skin, lemon butter, a cold glass of something white.

Ingredients (serves 2)

  • 2 salmon fillets, skin-on, 6–8 oz each
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 sprigs fresh dill or thyme · 1 lemon
  • Lemon Dill Butter Sauce:
  • 3 tbsp cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 shallot, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped · 1 tsp parsley
  1. Pat fillets completely dry. Season flesh side with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat cast iron over medium-high until very hot, ~3 min. Add oil.
  3. Place salmon skin-side down. Press gently with spatula for 10 sec to prevent curling.
  4. Cook skin-side down 4–5 min without moving — skin will release naturally when ready and be crispy.
  5. Flip. Add butter, garlic, and dill/thyme to pan. Baste with foaming butter 1–2 min.
  6. Pull at 120–125°F internal — just opaque at edges, slightly translucent at center. Rest 2 min.
  1. Lemon dill sauce (while salmon rests): Add shallot to same pan over medium, cook 1 min. Add lemon juice, scrape up any bits. Swirl in cold butter cubes one at a time until emulsified and glossy. Stir in dill and parsley. Season.

Filet Mignon

Thu May 28 · Last Night · Reverse Sear · Red Wine Pan Sauce

Last night. Just the two of you. Nine days in the mountains, ending the way it should — with something worth the occasion. Make it count.

Ingredients (serves 2)

  • 2 filet mignon, 6–8 oz each, 1.5–2" thick
  • Kosher salt + fresh cracked pepper
  • 1 tbsp neutral high-heat oil
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter + 2 sprigs thyme (basting)
  • Red Wine Pan Sauce:
  • 2 shallots, minced · 1 garlic clove, smashed
  • ¼ cup dry red wine (Pinot Noir or Merlot)
  • 1 cup beef stock
  • 2–3 tbsp cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1–2 sprigs fresh thyme · ½ tsp Dijon mustard (optional)

Compound Butter (made tonight in Fort Collins)

  • 3 tbsp softened unsalted butter · 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, minced · ½ tsp fresh thyme
  • Zest of ½ lemon · fleur de sel + fresh cracked pepper

Combine, roll in plastic wrap into a log, refrigerate until firm. Slice coins to serve.

Method — Reverse Sear

  1. Remove steaks from cooler 15 min ahead. Pat dry. Season generously with kosher salt all over. Pepper goes on after the sear.
  2. Preheat oven to 225°F. Place cast iron in oven as it preheats.
  3. Place steaks on a rack over a sheet pan. Insert thermometer probe if you have one.
  4. Bake at 225°F until internal temp reads 110–115°F — approximately 25–35 min depending on thickness. Check at 20 min for thin cuts.
  5. Remove from oven. Rest 2–3 min while heating cast iron on stovetop over high until smoking.
  6. Add 1 tbsp neutral oil to the screaming-hot skillet. Place steaks in pan.
  7. Sear 1.5–2 min per side — flip every 15–20 sec for a perfectly even crust.
  8. Add 2 tbsp butter, thyme, and an optional smashed garlic clove. Tilt pan and baste continuously for 30–45 sec.
  9. Season tops with fresh cracked pepper. Transfer to a warm plate. Tent loosely with foil. Rest 5–8 min minimum.

Final Temp 130–135°F After sear + rest

Red Wine Pan Sauce (while steaks rest)

  1. Do not wipe out the pan — the brown bits on the bottom are flavor.
  2. Add minced shallots to the hot pan over medium. Sauté 1–2 min until softened.
  3. Add thyme and garlic; cook 30 sec.
  4. Deglaze with ¼ cup red wine — scrape the fond off the pan bottom with a wooden spoon.
  5. Add beef stock. Bring to a simmer, reduce by half over 5–7 min (~½ cup remaining).
  6. Remove from heat. Whisk in cold butter cubes one at a time until sauce is silky and glossy.
  7. Add Dijon if using. Season with salt and pepper. Spoon over rested steaks immediately.

  • 1.5 tbsp olive oil · ½ tsp kosher salt · ¼ tsp cracked pepper

  1. Toss asparagus with oil, salt, pepper on a sheet pan. Single layer.

Troubleshooting

Tap a cook to expand. Tap a question for the fix.

🍔 Smash Burgers
Cast iron not hot enough
A water droplet should vanish instantly — if it just sizzles and sits there, give it 2 more minutes. A pan that's not screaming hot will steam the patties instead of crust them, and the whole technique falls apart.
🥩 Chuck Roast
Roast won't shred at 3 hours
Put it back. Cover, return to 275°F for 20–30 min increments. It's not done until two forks pull it apart with almost no resistance. Under-braised roast is tough; more time only makes it more tender.
Mashed potatoes are gluey or pasty
Over-mixed, or cold dairy was added. You can't fully fix gluey potatoes, but folding in warm cream and a knob of butter will help. Next time: steam-dry after draining, warm the dairy before adding, and stop folding as soon as it's creamy.
🐟 Salmon Day
Pellicle isn't forming after 2 hours
Give it another 30–60 min. If the cabin is humid, crack a window or move the fan closer. The surface needs to feel tacky and dry to the touch — not wet or shiny. Smoking without a pellicle won't ruin it, smoke just won't adhere as evenly.
Smoked salmon temp rising too fast
Check your Traeger — it may be running hot. Drop it to 150°F and crack the lid briefly to bleed off heat. The slow 3–4 hour smoke is what builds the candied exterior; rushing it produces drier, less flavorful fish.
🥩 T-Bone Steaks
Steaks cooking unevenly (one side more done)
T-bones are naturally uneven — the strip side cooks faster than the tenderloin side. Rotate the pan in the oven halfway through, or tent the tenderloin side with a small piece of foil while the strip catches up.
Pan sauce looks greasy and separated
Remove from heat immediately. Add 1 tbsp cold water and whisk hard. Or drop in one more cube of cold butter and swirl — cold butter re-emulsifies a broken sauce most of the time.
🥩 Filet Mignon
Filet hit temp too fast in the 225°F oven
The oven is forgiving but thinner cuts (under 1.5") rise fast — check at 20 min next time. If you pulled too soon, the final sear will make up some ground. If overcooked, serve with extra pan sauce — it rescues a lot.
